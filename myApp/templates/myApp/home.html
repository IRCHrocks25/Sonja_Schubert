The moment that changed everything

Portrait Image

Sonja Christina

Emotional Companion & Journey Practitioner

It was in a hospital room, watching someone I loved navigate the final days of their cancer journey, that I realized something profound: the medical system could treat the disease, but it couldn't hold the person.

I saw the gap—the space between diagnosis and peace, between treatment and transformation, between being a patient and being fully human.

As a nurse with over 25 years of experience, I'd been trained to manage symptoms, monitor vitals, and follow protocols. But what I witnessed in that room was something deeper: the need for emotional presence, for someone to hold space for the fear, the grief, the questions that have no medical answers.

Not more treatment. Deeper connection.

That moment changed everything. It led me to become a Certified Journey Practitioner, to study trauma-informed care, and to create a practice dedicated entirely to the emotional and spiritual dimensions of a cancer diagnosis.

Today, I offer the space I once needed myself. A place where women can come exactly as they are—scared, angry, hopeful, exhausted, all of it. A place where they don't have to be strong, positive, or productive. A place where they can simply be.

This is not about fixing. It's about presence. It's about coming home to yourself, even in the midst of the storm.
